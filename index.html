How to use the Circle of Fifths
  • Click any segment to explore that key — see its chords, key signature, and borrowed-chord colours.
  • Click "Set as Key" in the info drawer to apply it as your active key.
  • Shift-click two segments to compare keys — shared chords and distance between them.
  • Outer ring = Major keys · Inner ring = their relative Minor keys.
  • Adjacent segments share the most notes and move smoothly; opposite segments create maximum contrast.
  • Numbered badges outside the ring show which chord in your progression lands on that key.
  • Use the ◀ / ▶ Transpose buttons below to shift your whole progression by a 4th or 5th.

How to Use Fretty

Click frets on the guitar neck to build chords. Add them to your progression and watch the key and theory panels update in real time.

  • Click frets to select notes — Fretty identifies the chord automatically.
  • + Add to Progression to build a chord sequence.
  • Click a chord chip to reload its voicing onto the fretboard.
  • Explore Theory opens the scale degree and theory modal.
  • Diatonic Chords shows chords that fit your detected key.
  • Next Chord Ideas suggests musically interesting next moves.
